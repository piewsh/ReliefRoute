Safety Protocols for Disaster Prevention

Avalanche
  • Avoid areas prone to avalanches during high-risk times.
  • Carry transceivers, probes, and shovels.
  • Travel in groups; watch out for each other.
  • Heed warning signs: recent snowfall, wind-loading.
Heatwave
  • Stay hydrated; avoid peak sun hours.
  • Use fans or AC to stay cool.
  • Check on elderly and children.
  • Wear lightweight, light-colored clothing.
Flood
  • Evacuate to higher ground if needed.
  • Never walk or drive through floodwaters.
  • Shut off utilities when instructed.
  • Keep an emergency flood kit ready.
Hurricane
  • Follow local evacuation orders.
  • Board up windows; secure outdoor items.
  • Stay indoors during the storm.
  • Have a family communication plan.
Landslide
  • Avoid vulnerable slopes during heavy rain.
  • Watch for cracks or unusual sounds.
  • Evacuate if authorities advise.
  • Keep emergency supplies ready.
Blizzard
  • Stay home; avoid travel.
  • Stock food, water, blankets.
  • Dress in warm layers if you go out.
  • Watch for frostbite and hypothermia.
Cyclone
  • Evacuate if advised by authorities.
  • Secure loose objects and reinforce windows.
  • Stay indoors during the storm.
  • Listen to reliable weather updates.
Tsunami
  • Evacuate immediately if in a zone.
  • Move inland or to higher ground.
  • Stay away from coasts and low areas.
  • Follow official emergency alerts.
Volcano
  • Follow evacuation orders promptly.
  • Stay indoors; seal windows/doors.
  • Wear masks against ash inhalation.
  • Monitor official volcanic updates.
Earthquake
  • Drop, cover, and hold on.
  • Stay away from windows and heavy objects.
  • Have an emergency kit ready.
  • Identify safe spots in each room.
Drought
  • Fix leaks and reduce water use.
  • Adhere to burn and water restrictions.
  • Plant drought-resistant vegetation.
  • Monitor and report water issues.
Tornado
  • Shelter in a sturdy building or underground.
  • Stay away from windows and doors.
  • If outdoors, lie flat in a low area.
  • Have a household tornado plan.
